### 3. Top 5 Strategic Gaps to Fill

| Priority | Gap | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | **Socratic AI Tutoring** | AskAide AI's RAG-based Q&A is not a true tutoring experience. Vidhya AI, Edzy, and Khanmigo guide students through problems rather than giving answers. This is the #1 feature requested in school AI pilots. Implementing a multi-turn Socratic dialogue agent would close the largest experience gap. |
| **2** | **Parent-Teacher Communication** | Schools purchasing B2B licenses expect parent engagement features. AskAide AI now has a Parent Dashboard (multi-child, real-time progress, streaks, activity feed), but parent-teacher communication (messaging, meeting scheduling) is still missing. Byju's and Super Tutor offer parent dashboards but no competitor offers direct parent-teacher messaging — a blue ocean opportunity. |
| **3** | **Multi-Language Support** | Indian K12 education happens in 22+ regional languages. Obi and Khan Academy have demonstrated broad language support is feasible. AskAide AI's English-only interface excludes the majority of the target market. This is critical for B2B school adoption outside metro cities. |
| **4** | **Offline Support** | Many Indian schools have intermittent connectivity. PhysicsWallah, Byju's, and Doubtnut support offline access. Without it, AskAide AI is unusable in low-connectivity environments where the need for AI-powered learning tools is highest. |
| **5** | **LMS / Google Classroom Integration** | Schools already use Google Classroom and other LMS platforms. SparkSchool AI, QuestionWell, and AceQuiz offer direct integration. Without LMS sync, teachers face friction adopting AskAide AI alongside their existing workflow. |
